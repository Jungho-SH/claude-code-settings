# Know When to Quit

A ship without direction finds every wind to be a headwind.

Holding on because of sunk time is not research.
But giving up at the first sign of trouble isn't either. Give it a fair shot. "Fair" is hard to define globally, but at minimum, try enough to convince yourself.

## When to Keep Going

- Each attempt teaches you something new. Results aren't there yet, but understanding is deepening.
- You can explain why it's not working, and you have a basis for what to try differently next.
- You're narrowing down the failure. Eliminating possibilities.

## When to Stop

- You don't know why it's failing but keep changing numbers anyway. A well-reasoned sweep is an experiment; randomly poking parameters is prayer.
- "I've come this far" is the only reason to continue. That's not evidence — that's attachment.
- You've tried multiple angles and keep hitting the same wall. Question the direction, not just the method.

## Watch for Inertia

If you're repeating the same thing on autopilot, stop and think.
If it's not working, look at it from a different angle. Read papers. Ask someone.
If you don't know, look it up. Grinding alone on the same dead end is the dumbest thing you can do.

## Don't Fear Opportunity Cost

Letting go feels like wasting the time already spent.
But while you're clinging to something that doesn't work, you're missing something that could.
The real waste isn't the time already spent — it's the time you'll spend going forward.
